Passport control

Do you qualify for DASA UG?

Eligibility rests on two independent checks that must both pass: your nationality/residency category, and your academic record. Miss either one and you're disqualified — at any stage, even after seat allotment.

Check 1 of 2

Nationality & residency category

DASA is open only to the categories below. Regular Indian students who studied entirely in India go through JoSAA, not DASA.

1Foreign Nationals (FN)

Citizens of any country other than India, holding a valid passport issued by that country's government. No restriction on where you completed schooling — India or abroad.

2Overseas Citizens of India (OCI)

Valid OCI cardholders under the Citizenship Act, 1955. No residential restriction — apply from any country, including India. Conditions apply based on when your OCI card was issued, so check the official brochure's annexure for the exact cut-off.

3Persons of Indian Origin (PIO)

Valid PIO cardholders, treated on largely the same footing as OCI applicants for DASA purposes.

4NRIs & Indian nationals studying abroad

This category has the strictest conditions, since you hold an Indian passport but studied overseas. You must have:

  • ✓Studied abroad for a minimum of 2 years within the last 8 years immediately before your admission year
  • ✓Had Class XI and Class XII (or equivalent) fall within that 2-year abroad period
  • ✓Passed your qualifying (Class XII) examination from outside India
Special sub-quota

CIWG — Children of Indian Workers in the Gulf

A dedicated sub-category inside the DASA supernumerary quota (5% within the overall 15% DASA reservation), built for families working in the Gulf. Fees are pegged close to what Indian students pay — a major saving over the standard Non-SAARC rate.

CIWG conditions

To qualify, you need:

  • Either parent working, or having worked, in one of the 8 listed Gulf countries, with a valid employment visa/work permit
  • Class XI and Class XII completed entirely in that Gulf country
  • Same academic benchmarks as general DASA (PCM + minimum aggregate)
  • Born on or after the cut-off date specified in that year's brochure

CIWG applicants can also opt into general Non-CIWG seats — if allotted a general seat instead, the fee difference already paid is refunded per DASA rules.

Check 2 of 2

Academic & entrance exam requirements

Meeting your nationality category alone isn't enough — your school record and JEE Main rank both have to clear the bar.

Qualifying exam

Class XII or equivalent

Passed (or awaiting result of) Class XII / 10+2 or an equivalent examination from a system recognised by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU). Diploma-holders (10+3, with Maths compulsory) can also qualify under a separate path.

Subjects

Physics, Chemistry, Maths

PCM must be compulsory subjects in your qualifying exam. Some institutes accept Biology, Biotechnology, or Computer Science as a substitute for Chemistry for specific programs — check the individual institute's rules.

Minimum marks

75% aggregate / 7.5 CGPA

You need at least 75% aggregate marks, or an equivalent CGPA of 7.5 out of 10, in your qualifying examination.

Appearing candidates

Result by 30 September

Still in Class XII this year? You can still apply — but your final result must be declared by 30th September of the admission year, or your candidature is void.

Age

Born on/after the cut-off date

Each year's brochure sets a specific date-of-birth cut-off (e.g. "born on or after 1 October [year]"), taken from your Secondary Education Board / University certificate — not a passport. Always confirm the exact date for the current cycle.

Entrance exam

JEE Main is now compulsory

DASA UG dropped SAT subject scores — your valid JEE Main All India Rank (AIR) is now the sole basis for seat allotment, the same exam Indian applicants take.

Both conditions apply together, not either/or: A strong JEE Main rank does not waive the 75%/7.5 CGPA academic requirement, and vice versa. Falling short on either one at any verification stage — even after a seat is allotted — voids your admission.
