What is DASA

A Government of India scheme built to bring international students in

Direct Admission of Students Abroad (DASA) is an initiative of India's Ministry of Education, running since 2001, that reserves dedicated undergraduate engineering, architecture and planning seats at NITs, IIEST Shibpur, IIITs, SPAs and other centrally-funded technical institutes (Other-GFTIs) for students who've been educated outside the regular Indian schooling stream.

These seats are supernumerary — added on top of the regular intake — so DASA candidates aren't competing with Indian JEE Main / JoSAA applicants for the same seats. Admission runs on your JEE Main All India Rank, coordinated online by a rotating NIT (IIEST Shibpur for the 2026 cycle) rather than through JoSAA.

Good to know: DASA is separate from JoSAA/CSAB. If you already hold a JoSAA seat, you can still register for DASA as a fresh candidate — but accepting a new DASA seat automatically cancels your JoSAA allotment. IITs never participate in DASA; they're reached only through JEE Advanced.
Who this is for

Four passenger categories, one boarding gate

DASA groups applicants into categories based on nationality and where they studied. Your category decides your fee slab and which sub-quota you fall under.

Category 01

Foreign Nationals

Citizens of any country other than India, holding a valid foreign passport. No restriction on where you studied.

Category 02

OCI Cardholders

Overseas Citizens of India — eligible regardless of country of residence or study, subject to conditions on when your OCI card was issued.

Category 03

PIO Cardholders

Persons of Indian Origin holding a valid PIO card, treated similarly to OCI applicants under the scheme.

Category 04

NRIs studying abroad

Indian nationals who completed at least 2 years abroad within the last 8 years — including grades XI and XII — with the qualifying exam passed overseas.

Sub-quota

CIWG

Children of Indian Workers in the Gulf — a special sub-category within DASA for children of Indian citizens employed in UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Oman, Kuwait, Bahrain, Iraq or Iran, with lower fees.

Split by region

SAARC vs Non-SAARC

Applicants from SAARC countries get a 50% tuition waiver if their qualifying exam was passed in a SAARC nation. Everyone else falls under the Non-SAARC fee slab.

The journey, at a glance

From JEE Main rank to hostel room, in five checkpoints

The full application and counselling process has more detail — but here's the shape of it.

Checkpoint 1

Sit JEE Main

DASA has no separate entrance test — your JEE Main All India Rank is what institutes see.

Checkpoint 2

Register & pay

Create your DASA profile online, upload documents, and pay the non-refundable registration fee.

Checkpoint 3

Fill & lock choices

Rank every institute-branch combination you'd accept, in order of real preference.

Checkpoint 4

Seat allotment rounds

Multiple rounds run automatically against your rank and choices — accept, float, or exit.

Checkpoint 5

Report & get your visa

Pay balance fees, verify documents, and (for foreign nationals) apply for your student visa.
