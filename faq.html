Questions applicants ask most

The things that come up again and again in DASA forums, WhatsApp groups, and counselling calls.

No. IITs never participate in DASA. The only route into an IIT is JEE Advanced, open to a limited number of foreign-national/OCI/PIO seats through a separate IIT admission process — not through DASA.
Yes, both are eligible categories under DASA, with no residential restriction. However, conditions apply based on when your OCI/PIO card was issued — check the relevant annexure in the official brochure to confirm you qualify.
CIWG (Children of Indian Workers in the Gulf) is a sub-category inside the DASA supernumerary quota, specifically for children of Indian citizens working in a listed Gulf country. CIWG candidates pay significantly lower tuition — close to what Indian students pay — versus the standard Non-SAARC rate.
Yes. CIWG candidates can opt into both CIWG and Non-CIWG seats. If eventually allotted a general seat instead of a CIWG one, the fee difference already paid is refunded as per DASA rules.
Yes, you can register for DASA as a fresh candidate even while holding a JoSAA (NIT+ system) seat. If DASA then allots you a new seat, your JoSAA seat is automatically cancelled — so only do this if you'd genuinely prefer the DASA outcome.
You become eligible for the spot round, which fills seats left vacant after the regular allotment rounds. Candidates who declined an earlier offer can also re-register for the spot round, generally by paying a fresh registration fee.
After. Foreign nationals use the provisional admission letter issued once a seat is allotted to apply for a student visa at the nearest Indian Mission. Start the visa process as soon as you have that letter — timelines vary a lot by country and can be tight against the reporting deadline.

Diaspora children may be eligible for scholarship support through schemes like the SPDC (Study in India / diaspora scholarship programs) — check the current listings on edcilindia.co.in and your allotted institute's financial aid office, since availability and eligibility change yearly.
